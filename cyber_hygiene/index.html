Strengthening My Digital Defenses

A Personal Cyber Hygiene Audit and Action Plan

1. Where I Stood: My Initial Audit

To improve my security, I first had to understand my weaknesses. Here's what I found during my self-assessment:

Passwords and Authentication:

  • My passwords were mostly strong and unique, but I wasn't updating them regularly.
  • I was already using a password manager and had MFA enabled on my most important accounts.

Device Security:

  • My main devices had active antivirus software and firewalls.
  • However, I realized I was occasionally neglecting to update some of my third-party apps.

Social Media Privacy:

  • While my profiles were set to private, I found that I was oversharing personal details and travel plans.
  • I also wasn't reviewing my privacy settings as often as I should have been.

Email Practices:

  • I felt confident in my ability to spot phishing attempts and verify email sources.
  • My main weakness here was a lack of automated email filtering rules.

Data Backup:

  • I had a decent backup routine: weekly to an external drive and monthly to the cloud.
  • Key finding: I discovered my local backup was not encrypted, leaving it vulnerable.

2. Understanding My Risks

Based on the audit, I evaluated each issue to prioritize what needed fixing first. This helped me focus my efforts on the most significant threats.

Risk Area Severity Likelihood My Priority
Outdated third-party apps High Medium High
Weak or reused passwords Medium High High
Oversharing on social media Medium Medium Medium
Unencrypted local backup Medium Medium Medium
Lack of email filters Low Medium Low

3. My Action Plan for Better Security

With a clear view of the risks, I created this actionable plan to strengthen my digital defenses. These are the commitments I'm making to myself.

Passwords and Authentication:

  • I will rotate all my passwords every 6 months using my password manager.
  • I will conduct an audit of my saved passwords to eliminate any duplicates.

Device Security:

  • I will set monthly reminders to check and update all my third-party applications.
  • I will enable automatic updates for applications wherever possible.

Social Media Privacy:

  • I will review and adjust my privacy settings on all platforms every quarter.
  • I will stop sharing travel or other sensitive updates in real time.

Email Practices:

  • I will implement email filters to automatically flag emails from unknown domains.
  • I will participate in a phishing simulation test quarterly to keep my skills sharp.

Data Backup:

  • I will encrypt my local backup drive using my OS's built-in encryption tools.
  • I will create and follow a weekly backup checklist to ensure I don't miss it.

4. Staying on Track: How I'll Monitor My Progress

A plan is only useful if you stick to it. Here’s how I’ll hold myself accountable:

  • I will use a spreadsheet to log my password changes, software updates, and backup status.
  • I have set calendar reminders for all my monthly and quarterly checks.
  • I plan to re-evaluate my cyber hygiene every three months to assess my progress and adjust the action plan as needed.
