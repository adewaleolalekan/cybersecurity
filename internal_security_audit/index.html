Internal Security Audit Report

An Assessment of Controls and Compliance at Botium Toys

1. Background

Botium Toys is a small U.S. business that develops and sells toys from a single physical location that serves as their main office, storefront, and warehouse. Recently, the company's online presence has grown significantly, attracting customers both domestically and internationally, including from the European Union (E.U.).

This rapid expansion has placed increasing pressure on the IT department to support a global online market securely. The IT manager has initiated this internal audit to address concerns about maintaining compliance and business operations without a clear security plan. The audit's goal is to identify and mitigate potential risks, threats, and vulnerabilities to critical assets, ensuring compliance with regulations like PCI DSS and GDPR.

2. Executive Summary

This report outlines the findings of the internal security audit. The assessment revealed a significant risk score of 8 out of 10 due to numerous missing security controls and gaps in compliance with major regulations. Key deficiencies were identified in access control, data protection, and disaster recovery. This report provides a detailed breakdown of the findings and offers prioritized recommendations to mitigate these risks and improve the company's overall security posture.

3. Scope and Goals of the Audit

Scope

The audit covers the entire security program at Botium Toys, including all on-premises and employee equipment, data storage, internal processes, and compliance practices related to its physical and online retail operations.

Goals

The primary goal is to assess existing assets and controls to identify deficiencies and determine which security and compliance best practices must be implemented to protect company assets, reduce risk, and ensure adherence to relevant regulations.

4. Controls Assessment Checklist

Control In Place? Explanation
Least Privilege No Currently, all employees have access to customer data; privileges need to be limited to reduce the risk of a breach.
Disaster Recovery Plans No There are no disaster recovery plans in place. These need to be implemented to ensure business continuity.
Password Policies No Employee password requirements are minimal, which could allow a threat actor to more easily access secure data.
Separation of Duties No Needs to be implemented to reduce the possibility of fraud/access to critical data.
Firewall Yes The existing firewall blocks traffic based on an appropriately defined set of security rules.
Intrusion Detection System (IDS) No The IT department needs an IDS in place to help identify possible intrusions by threat actors.
Backups No The IT department needs to have backups of critical data in case of a breach to ensure business continuity.
Antivirus Software Yes Antivirus software is installed and monitored regularly by the IT department.
Encryption No Encryption is not currently used; implementing it would provide greater confidentiality of sensitive information.
Physical Locks & CCTV Yes The store’s physical location has sufficient locks and functioning CCTV surveillance.

5. Compliance Assessment

PCI DSS (Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard)

  • Access to Cardholder Info: Fails. Currently, all employees have access to the company’s internal data.
  • Secure Environment: Fails. Credit card information is not encrypted and all employees have access to it.
  • Data Encryption Procedures: Fails. The company does not currently use encryption to ensure confidentiality.
  • Secure Password Policies: Fails. Password policies are nominal and no password management system is in place.

GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)

  • Data Privacy/Security: Fails. The company does not currently use encryption to ensure confidentiality of customer data.
  • Breach Notification: Pass. A plan is in place to notify E.U. customers within 72 hours of a data breach.
  • Data Classification & Inventory: Fails. Current assets have been inventoried but not classified.
  • Privacy Policies & Procedures: Pass. Policies and procedures have been developed and are enforced.

6. Recommendations

To improve Botium Toys’ security posture and ensure the confidentiality of sensitive information, multiple controls need to be implemented. To address gaps in compliance, the company must implement controls such as Least Privilege, separation of duties, and encryption. The company also needs to properly classify assets to identify additional controls that may be needed.

Priority 1: Critical

  • Implement Access Controls: Enforce Least Privilege and Separation of Duties to restrict access to sensitive data.
  • Deploy Encryption: Encrypt sensitive data, especially customer financial information.
  • Establish Backups & DR Plan: Create disaster recovery plans and implement regular data backups.

Priority 2: High

  • Strengthen Password Policy: Update password policies and implement a password management system.
  • Install an IDS: Deploy an Intrusion Detection System to identify intrusions.
  • Formalize Legacy System Management: Establish a regular schedule and clear procedures for maintaining legacy systems.
  • Classify Assets: Properly classify all inventoried assets to better identify risks.
